what_i_learned: |

 Today was a highly analytical and strategic day focused on wrapping up complex experiment evaluations and translating those deep technical metrics into clear, high-level messaging. I continued running core experiments outlined in our research manual, executing critical validation tracks to stress-test our framework. Specifically, I analyzed and evaluated the results across three massive diagnostic milestones. Trust Hierarchy Validation: Verifying how securely the model routes decisions up the neural framework under perturbed conditions to guarantee clinical safety. PPG Failure Mode Analysis: Rigorously isolating the exact boundaries where mechanical pulse volume signals break down due to artifact noise, and mapping how our system handles these specific sensor dropouts. Multimodal vs. Unimodal Drift Advantage: Quantifying the exact performance gap to mathematically prove how much more resilient our fused ECG/PPG model is at resisting data drift compared to single-signal baselines. Documenting the exact advantage our multimodal model holds over unimodal baselines gives us an airtight, bulletproof argument that will serve as the centerpiece for our final paper. We are no longer just theorizing about robustness; we have the empirical visualizations to back it up. Beyond the raw work, we pivoted into communication strategy. We drafted and refined our elevator pitch, distilling our highly technical, cross-dataset multi-domain framework into a powerful, concise narrative for non-specialist audiences. In tandem, we returned to Overleaf to weave these new experimental results, charts, and failure mode insights into the body of our final research paper, keeping our foundational benchmarks rock-solid.
