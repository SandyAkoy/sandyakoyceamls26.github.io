 Today was an incredible day of learning, combining hands-on technical workshops with deeper model experimentation. I participated in Part 2 of Dr. Dacon’s "Introduction to Data Analysis" workshop. This session focused on data cleaning and feature engineering, followed by step-by-step coding to correctly visualize data using Matplotlib and Seaborn. I wrote code to generate several visual summaries, including scatterplots and heatmaps, which made the data much easier to interpret. We then put these skills to the test by training several traditional classifiers on the breast cancer dataset, comparing their performance using Accuracy and F1-score. It was incredibly insightful to see how different algorithms handled the features, requiring a sharp eye for detail to understand the nuances in the metrics. In tandem with the workshop, I continued executing experiments for my own multimodal research to generate a comprehensive suite of plots. I focused on structuring comparisons, model analysis, Trust Hierarchy Validation, and Complementary feature domain (CFD) contributions. A major highlight was computing our final Complementarity Reliability Gain. Not only did it clear our target threshold of 0.05, but it reached an impressive 0.0938. This exceptional score firmly validates our ECG and PPG domain pairing, proving that our complementary fusion significantly reduces redundancy and preserves high DCA (.8977) under benign perturbations.
