what_i_learned: |

   Today was an incredibly interactive day that perfectly combined deep, hands-on algorithmic analysis with inspiring professional exposure and collaborative reviews. I spent the first half of the day focused on the MIMIC dataset to gather the final experimental results required to complete our comprehensive CFD Discovery Assignment. This involved systematically answering a detailed questionnaire designed to our model's performance and fairness. Identifying Complementary Pairs: mapping and evaluating the top 5 and bottom 5 complementary signal pairs across our merged domains to observe where the information gain is strongest or weakest. Robustness Testing: evaluating the model's structural threshold under varying levels of noise. Perturbation Analysis: running manual-style signal corruptions to determine how gracefully our latent space representation degrades. Dynamic Domain Importance: evaluating whether our ECG and PPG domains dynamically shift in feature importance depending on which partner signal they are paired with during training. Case Studies: providing concrete clinical examples for every single prompt in the questionnaire. After completing the assignment, our cohort transitioned to an incredibly engaging zoom session with guest speaker Dr. Chris Crawford. Dr. Crawford, a professor at University of Alabama whose interest lay within the human-technology Interaction, shared his fascinating background in robotics and physiological computing. He detailed his research across human-computer interaction (HCI), brain-computer interface (BCI), electrophysiology, computer science education, and drone-racing systems controlled by the brain. Following Dr. Crawford's presentation, we spent a portion watching the recorded elevator pitches of the other research groups in our cohort. We had a great time watching everyone's presentations, laughing at the creative hooks, and celebrating the highly engaging ways our peers translated their complex projects. To close out the day, I went back into our Overleaf directory to add finer details to the CFD discovery report.

reflection: |

   Reflecting on today, the entire experience was both incredibly fun and highly interactive. Completing the CFD Discovery Assignment gave me a much deeper appreciation for how our fused model handles noise. Physically mapping out the top 5 and bottom 5 complementary pairs made me realize that our feature layers aren't just reacting blindly; they are making smart, sound tradeoffs to keep our predictive accuracy stable. Exploring whether a domain's importance shifts depending on its partner was incredibly eye-opening and taught me how dynamic deep neural networks can be when dealing with multimodal fusion. Meeting Dr. Chris Crawford was an absolute highlight of the week. I was completely unaware of how brain-computer interfaces could be merged with robotics and drone racing so seamlessly, and it opened my eyes to an entirely new dimension of physiological computing. It was incredibly inspiring to hear how he uses electrophysiology such as EEG to build systems that adapt to cognitive states. The session left me with fresh perspectives on how our own work with ECG and PPG signals fits into the wider, incredibly exciting future of human-centered AI. Watching the other groups' elevator pitches afterwards was the perfect social wrapper for the day. Seeing the clever and funny ways my peers made their complex science relatable made me feel so proud to be part of this cohort. It felt great to laugh, celebrate their hard work, and see how much talent is packed into this program. With the CFD assignment practically wrapped up, I am incredibly eager to see the absolute final results of our multi-dataset comparison.
