reflection: |

   Reflecting on today, the shift toward our paper outline on Overleaf makes the culmination of this summer's research feel incredibly tangible. We are no longer just running isolated experiments; we are actively crafting the narrative that will introduce our multimodal pipeline. The pairing game we played this afternoon was fantastic. Beyond the laughs and seeing how hilariously fast we had to switch mindsets, it was a subtle, powerful lesson in perspective-taking. In research, just like in that game, you have to completely step outside your own biases to truly understand someone else's standpoint whether that person is a teammate or a peer presenting a different approach. Watching Mercy during SAIRI Connect provided an incredible interpretation. Since my research focuses so heavily on CFD, Trust Hierarchy Validation, and Explainable AI (XAI), viewing a cinematic dystopia where an AI judge relies blindly on data without transparent safeguards really hit home. It sparked a lot of deep thoughts about how vital it is for machine learning engineers to build systems that prevent safety violations and remain explainable, rather than treating AI as a flawless "black box." It reinforced that the fairness checks we are implementing in our cardiovascular signal model aren't just technical metrics, they are ethical necessities.
