   Today was a highly focused, academic day dedicated to expanding and refining our project's theoretical foundation through literature reviews. I systematically evaluated 9 research papers, carefully analyzing their abstracts, structural methodologies, and experimental studies. For each paper, I strictly focused on extracting four core elements to benchmark against my own work: Data Output & Results: Auditing the specific performance metrics (such as Accuracy, F1-score, and AUC) implemented across various cardiovascular datasets. Methodological Approach: Breaking down how the authors structured their signal processing and feature pipelines. Multimodal Data Fusion: Studying how other researchers technically handle the intersection of distinct domains. Explainable AI (XAI): Investigating how these studies implemented interpretability mechanisms (like feature attributions) to provide clear, traceable reasons behind a model's clinical decisions. In tandem with analyzing these new papers, I conducted a thorough retro-review of the literature reviews I wrote during the earlier portion of the institute. I audited my past drafts to spot gaps, refine technical terminology, and implement critical structural changes. This ensures that our final papers will seamlessly marry our outstanding empirical metrics DCA, EDC, and our Complementarity Reliability Gain with a robust, highly updated scientific narrative.

reflection: |

   Reflecting on today, transitioning from active pipeline to deep-dive reading gave me a completely new appreciation for our model's uniqueness. Reviewing 9 papers made it clear that while many researchers utilize multimodal fusion, very few concurrently solve model drift while maintaining Explainable AI (XAI) standards. Seeing how other frameworks break down their black-box models to show the specific reasons behind a clinical decision reinforced exactly why our Trust Hierarchy Validation and CFD contribution plots are so vital. Revisiting and editing the literature reviews I wrote weeks ago was also a great exercise in self-assessment. It was amazing to see how much more intuitive the technical jargon has become. Early on, concepts like cross-modal feature alignment felt abstract; today, I was able to easily spot areas in my old writing that needed more precision and rewrite them with complete confidence. Taking the time to patch up past mistakes and build on top of my earlier work shows how much my perspective has matured throughout this institute. I feel like our paper's narrative is just as powerful as the data supporting it.
