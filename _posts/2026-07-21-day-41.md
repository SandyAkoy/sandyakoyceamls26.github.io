   Today was a profound milestone in our summer journey as I dedicated time to crafting, and polishing our final SAIRI research presentation. Assembling the slides for our final showcase required synthesizing every single phase of our work into a cohesive, high-impact narrative. Evaluation of methodology from our early signal preprocessing routines to our advanced cross-dataset evaluations using the PTB-XL, BIDMC, and MIMIC datasets. We thoroughly reviewed every structural pillar of our research paper to ensure our presentation accurately reflects the full synopsis of our project: Methodology & Machine Learning Pipelines: highlighting our dynamic feature extraction across time, frequency, and time-frequency domains alongside our pyCFD 3.0 cross-modal discovery across all 15 domain pairs. Empirical Results: showcasing our optimized Drift Classification Accuracy (DCA), our Energy Drift Correlation (EDC), and our peak Complementary Reliability Gain (CRG) under strict, patient-disjoint testing. System Biases & Ethical Safeguards: Detailing how our (CFD) framework identifies and mitigates latent algorithmic biases. Model Limitations & Failure Modes: clearly explaining our PPG Failure Mode Analysis and demonstrating how our reserved respiratory (RESP) channel acts as a dynamic arbitration layer during signal corruptions. In-Depth Data Analysis: presenting clear visual evidence including correlation heatmaps, perturbation plots, and failure mode breakdowns. To ensure our academic narrative was completely seamless, I spent time cross-referencing our presentation slides against the literature reviews we authored and the final draft of our research paper on Overleaf. This deep-dive review reinforced exactly how our system leverages Artificial Intelligence for the early prediction and risk stratification of cardiovascular disease using continuous ECG, PPG, and RESP signals.
