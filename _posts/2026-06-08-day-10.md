    Today, I continued expanding my understanding and knowledge of artificial intelligence applications in cardiovascular disease research by examining how different multimodal learning addresses signal analysis. My work focused on exploring three central research questions: identifying which ECG and PPG domains provide truly complementary information, determining when a cardiovascular AI model should trust or reconsider its predictions, and evaluating whether CFD-guided multimodal fusion preserves clinically meaningful physiological morphology better than traditional fusion techniques. I also generated heat maps, fusion graphs, and CFD index bar graphs that visualized correlations that showed patterns across signal domains. These visualizations highlighted areas where information overlapped as well as regions where each modality contributed unique physiological information. The heat maps and many other generative graphs I made served as an effective tool for identifying complementary signal characteristics that could improve multimodal learning performance.
